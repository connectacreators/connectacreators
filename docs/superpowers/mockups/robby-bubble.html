Robby — visual directions

Six options rendered on the real Connecta dashboard background. Hover each to feel the interaction.

0 — Current
REFERENCE
What you have today. Dark slate circle, fingerprint logo, red count badge. The "every SaaS support chat" archetype.
A — Cream / gold pill
AI IDENTITY
Why this one. Hand-drawn gold outline matches the AI bubbles inside the chat surface. Robby reads as one continuous AI identity across the app, not a generic widget. Glass-dark fill so it lifts off the page without shouting.
B — Cyan brand pill
PRIMARY COLOR
Brand-forward. Borrows your primary cyan, treats Robby like a system action button. Bolder, more product-like. Loses the "AI feel" — looks like a CTA.
C — Glass / frosted
RECEDES
Apple-style. Backdrop-blur, almost invisible. Best when you want the page content to dominate. Risk: too quiet — new users may not notice it.
D — Solid dark, minimal
BORING ON PURPOSE
Boring on purpose. Same card color as everything else. Disappears into the page rhythm. The count dot is the only signal something is alive.
E — Ambient orb (no pill)
ALTERNATIVE
If you change your mind on the pill. No hard container at all — just a glowing logo with a soft halo that pulses. Magical, but ambiguous: new users may not realize it's clickable.
F — Card with last nudge
LOUDEST
Robby 4
"Two things need your attention right now…"
Useful but heavy. Surfaces Robby's last nudge as a preview, so users don't have to click to know why it's pulsing. Great for engagement, but takes more space and competes with page content.
