Five professional Waves.

Same gesture, five design directions. Each iteration is shown three ways: full size on light, full size on dark, and at favicon scale so you can see how the silhouette holds up small.

Iteration 01 · refined silhouette

The Considered Wave

Same hand idea, all the doodly bits fixed. Four fingers in natural length order (middle tallest, pinky shortest), one continuous palm-to-wrist contour, no decorative motion lines. The silhouette tells the whole story.

Construction
Single closed path. Cubic Béziers on every curve.
Rationale
Kills the cartoon look from the draft. Reads as a hand at 16px.

Iteration 02 · geometric

The Bauhaus Wave

Built entirely from rectangles with consistent corner radius. Five capsules + one rounded-square palm. The shape relationships are mathematical, not gestural — feels like a stamp, not a sketch.

Construction
5 rounded rects (all r=7) + 1 rounded rect (r=22). Six primitives total.
Rationale
Scales infinitely. No curve drift between sizes. Reads as deliberate.

Iteration 03 · stroke-only

The Outline Wave

Same path as #51, but as a stroke instead of a fill. Architectural. Reads as a line-art tattoo — works on apparel, embroidered as merch, etched on a coffee cup. At favicon size, stroke weight scales up to maintain readability.

Construction
Single stroked path, weight 7px @ 120-unit grid (≈5.8% of width).
Rationale
Most versatile across surfaces. Inverts to outline-on-dark cleanly.

Iteration 04 · motion gesture

The Wave with a Trail

The hand from #51, now with a single elegant motion arc to its left. Replaces the three cartoon "speed lines" with one calligraphic curl — wisprflow-coded. Reads as movement without literally being movement marks.

Construction
Two paths: a stroked arc (left) + the filled hand silhouette (right). Aspect 16:14.
Rationale
Adds personality and direction without losing professionalism.

Iteration 05 · negative space

The Inverse Wave

Solid square with the hand carved out as negative space. The wave isn't the figure — the wave is the absence. The square IS the logo. Most graphic, most ownable, most "this is a tile in your favicon ladder."

Construction
Single path using fill-rule="evenodd" — outer square minus hand silhouette.
Rationale
The frame creates the mark. Works as app icon out of the box.
