Hands & gestures

The Almost-Touch

Two hands extending from opposite sides, fingertips almost meeting in the middle. Sistine Chapel rebuilt for creator + audience. The space between is the point.

— the gap is where the spark lives.

The Open Palm

A single hand, open, facing forward. Offering. Inviting. Receiving. The most fundamental human gesture — yes, please, here's mine.

— here, take this strategy.
39
Hands

The Heart Hands

The gen-z gesture — two thumbs touching, two index fingers up, forming a heart. Affection, agreement, "this is the one." Of-the-moment but timeless.

— the gesture every creator already does.
40
Hands

The Handshake

Two hands clasped in the middle. The oldest signal of agreement. Brand deals, partnerships, "we shook on it." Risky if generic — done with care, iconic.

— deal closed in two shapes.
41

The Reach

A single hand extending upward — palm rising, fingers raised. The mark of asking, of stretching toward, of "I want this." The creator's posture.

— growth is just reach, repeated.

The Wave

A hand held up, fingers slightly fanned, motion lines on the side. Hello, goodbye, "I see you." The friendliest interaction in human language.

— the first thing anyone wants: to be seen.

The Pointer
